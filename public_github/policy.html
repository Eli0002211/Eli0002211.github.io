• Safeguarding Policy

    INFERNO KARATE takes safeguarding concerns seriously: all instructors are DBS Enhanced cleared and undergo safeguarding training. The aim of the INFERNO KARATE is to enable our members to develop to their maximum potential in a safe and supportive environment. The following are minimum standards of safeguarding necessary to provide such an environment for children and vulnerable adults:

    (a) Instruction must take place in an “open” environment. Parents and carers should be able to observe the classes (albeit subject to conditions, such as a requirement that they do not interfere in the running of the lesson). Private situations behind closed doors are to be avoided. Children should not be encouraged to have “secret” discussions with adults in the club (although, this is different when a child is making a confidential disclosure- see (h));

    (b) All members must be treated equally with regard to courtesy, support, encouragement and the opportunity to ask questions. Children should be encouraged to raise questions when they have them and to report anything which is worrying them;

    (c) All members must be treated with dignity. Reducing a member to tears or humiliating a member as a means of discipline are inappropriate. Member to member bullying is also inappropriate;

    (d) The welfare of a child member is paramount above winning or achieving goals. For example, it is not appropriate to encourage a child of normal size and weight for their age to attempt dieting in order to make a lighter weight;

    (e) Whilst winning or achieving success is both laudable and to be encouraged, this is never to be done by sacrificing the overriding aim of child protection which is the welfare of the child It is the firm policy of INFERNO KARATE that a child’s welfare is enhanced by establishing in them an understanding of and commitment to fair play, enjoying sport, being modest in success and sporting in failure. INFERNO KARATE will not tolerate any behaviour at tournaments, gradings or other events which contradicts this policy;

    (f) Instructors and professional staff may only make physical contact with children and vulnerable adults where necessary for safe technique execution and where the member has been warned in advance.

    (g) Instructors and professional service staff are role models for children and vulnerable adults and must act as examples by not engaging in unprofessional or inappropriate activity while in the workplace;

    (h) Instructors and professional staff must not make sexual comments or engage in sexual behaviour towards or in front of any member;

    (i) Instructors and professional service staff should work in collaboration with parents, carers, and other agencies where appropriate. Instructors must inform the Safeguarding Lead should any safeguarding issues arise.

    Safeguarding enquiries should be made to: safeguarding(at)infernokarate(dot)com

  • Privacy Policy

    INFERNO KARATE takes data protection concerns seriously: we are registered with the Information Commissioners Office (ICO) and all instructors undergo safeguarding training. This privacy notice tells you what to expect us to do with your personal information:

    Your Rights

    Under UK data protection law, we must have a “lawful basis” for collecting and using your personal information. There is a list of possible lawful bases in the UK GDPR. You can find out more about lawful bases on the ICO’s website.

    Which lawful basis we rely on may affect your data protection rights which are in brief set out below. You can find out more about your data protection rights and the exemptions which may apply on the ICO’s website:

    Your right of access - You have the right to ask us for copies of your personal information. You can request other information such as details about where we get personal information from and who we share personal information with. There are some exemptions which means you may not receive all the information you ask for.

    Your right to rectification - You have the right to ask us to correct or delete personal information you think is inaccurate or incomplete.

    Your right to erasure - You have the right to ask us to delete your personal information.

    Your right to restriction of processing - You have the right to ask us to limit how we can use your personal information.

    Your right to object to processing - You have the right to object to the processing of your personal data.

    Your right to data portability - You have the right to ask that we transfer the personal information you gave us to another organisation, or to you.

    Your right to withdraw consent – When we use consent as our lawful basis you have the right to withdraw your consent at any time.

    If you make a request, we must respond to you without undue delay and in any event within one month.

    To make a data protection rights request, please contact us using the following email address:

    Legal(at)infernokarate(dot)com
